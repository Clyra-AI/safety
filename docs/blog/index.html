AI Engineering Operating Notes

This 10-part CAISI series is written for heads of AppSec, security engineering leaders, VP Engineering, and platform teams that need a practical model for governed autonomy. The throughline is simple: AI engineering is not about better prompting. It is about control, determinism, safe automation, and higher engineering throughput.

Posts 1-3 reframe the problem Posts 4-7 define the operating model Posts 8-10 define trust and maturity

How to read the series

Every post starts with an operational pain point, names the anti-pattern, introduces the better system pattern, explains why security cares, explains why platform and engineering care, shows a concrete artifact or workflow, and ends with a practical next step.

See the research | Open repository

Recurring themes

Systems over prompting

The unit of analysis is not prompt quality. It is the system that turns work into changes, approvals, evidence, and recovery paths.

Security and velocity can align

When workflows are designed correctly, the same controls that reduce blast radius also remove ambiguity and scale throughput.

Deterministic code owns deterministic steps

The best agent workflows interleave reasoning with scripts, validators, and policies that do not depend on model behavior.

Proof is part of the product

A governed system produces a reviewable packet from trigger to merge. Passing CI is necessary, but never sufficient.
