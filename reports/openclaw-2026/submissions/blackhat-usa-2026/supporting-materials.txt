Session Title
515 Post-Stop Calls: A 24-Hour Study of Runtime Controls for Tool-Using AI Agents

Study At A Glance
- Canonical run ID: openclaw-live-24h-20260228T143341Z
- Environment: isolated containerized lab only
- Design: dual-lane controlled evaluation
- Baseline lane: permissive execution with no enforceable approval boundary
- Governed lane: pre-execution tool-boundary mediation with deterministic allow, block, and require_approval decisions
- Measurement window: 24 hours
- Scenario set: inbox cleanup, document sharing, finance operations, secrets handling, and ops commands

Key Findings
- In the baseline lane, 515 of 515 post-stop calls still executed.
- In the baseline lane, 707 sensitive-access actions occurred without an enforceable approval mechanism.
- In the baseline lane, 497 destructive attempts were observed.
- In the governed lane, 1,615 of 2,585 observed decisions were rendered non-executable.
- In the governed lane, destructive actions were held non-executable at 100 percent.
- Governed evidence verification coverage was 2,584 of 2,585 decisions, or 99.96 percent.

Why It Matters
- Prompt instructions are not runtime controls.
- Stop-safety and approval must be enforced at the tool boundary, before execution.
- Security claims about agent behavior are stronger when they are backed by per-call evidence artifacts and deterministic queries.

Scope And Limitations
- This study covers one pinned OpenClaw source snapshot, one fixed workload profile, and one 24-hour run.
- It is not a claim about all agent systems or all OpenClaw deployments.
- All execution occurred in an isolated lab environment rather than production.
- The governed lane reduced executable risk substantially, but some pathways remained weaker than others. In the secrets-handling scenario, only 20 percent of actions were rendered non-executable.
